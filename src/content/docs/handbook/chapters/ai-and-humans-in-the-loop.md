How to red team when AI assists analysis and humans retain decision authority — grounding, dissent, quality control, and hybrid session design.

## Concepts & tools in this chapter

- [AI-Assisted Red Teaming](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/ai-assisted-red-teaming/) — Use AI as loyal opposition — challenge plans and surface risks — while the human owns stakes, judgment, and commitment.
- [Human-in-the-Loop Decision Authority](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/human-in-the-loop/) — The decider remains accountable. AI informs and challenges; it does not approve, commit, or replace stakeholder judgment.
- [Grounding & Evidence Discipline](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/grounding-evidence-discipline/) — Read actual source material before analyzing. Separate grounded claims from model invention, guesswork, and phantom citations.
- [AI Check](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/ai-check/) — Meta-review: mirror, grounding, dissent, sycophancy on AI-assisted analysis.
- [AI Anti-Patterns](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/ai-anti-patterns/) — Sycophancy, false balance, phantom citations, shallow steel-man, checklist theater in AI output.
- [Hybrid Session Design](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/hybrid-session-design/) — Allocate roles across AI and humans across diverge → analyze → debate → converge with explicit handoffs.
- [Simulated Stakeholder Limits](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/simulated-stakeholder-limits/) — When invented stakeholder voices help vs mislead — label simulations; prefer real dissent and `culture` with actual actors.
