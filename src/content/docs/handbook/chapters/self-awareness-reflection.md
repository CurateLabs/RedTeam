How red teamers understand themselves — cognition, temperament, emotion, communication — before engaging others.

## Concepts & tools in this chapter

- [Self-Authorship](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/self-authorship/) — Developmental capacity to internally define beliefs, identity, and relationships rather than relying on external validation.
- [Temperament / Personality Dimensions](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/temperament-personality-dimensions/) — Jungian-based typology for understanding how people prefer to perceive and decide — useful for anticipating group friction.
- [Emotional Intelligence Framework](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/emotional-intelligence-framework/) — Self-competencies and social competencies that affect how we receive and deliver challenge.
- [Interpersonal Communication](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/interpersonal-communication/) — How messages are sent, received, and distorted — foundation for productive devil's advocacy without personal attack.
- [Interpersonal Conflict](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/interpersonal-conflict/) — Patterns of disagreement escalation; separate task conflict (healthy) from relationship conflict (destructive).
- [Introspection](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/introspection/) — Deliberate inward examination — journaling, reflection time, structured exercises.
- [Who Am I?](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/who-am-i/) — Introspective exercise: articulate identity, values, and biases before engaging others.
