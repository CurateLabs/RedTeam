Defines red teaming, the four core principles, and how techniques are organized in RedTeam.

## Concepts & tools in this chapter

- [Red Teaming](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/red-teaming/) — A structured cognitive approach that challenges plans and decisions to surface hidden risks, unseen options, and flawed assumptions before commitment.
- [UFMCS & Handbook v9 (historical)](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/ufmcs-historical/) — University of Foreign Military and Cultural Studies — the defunct U.S. Army program that published *The Red Team Handbook* v9.0, the predecessor to Handbook v10.
- [Four Core Principles](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/four-core-principles/) — ACT, cultural empathy, GTM, and self-awareness — the organizing frame for all commands.
- [Applied Critical Thinking (ACT)](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/applied-critical-thinking/) — Deliberately analyze how we perceive, interpret, and reason — make implicit assumptions explicit and test them.
- [Cultural Empathy](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/cultural-empathy/) — Understand how cultural frames shape what others see, value, and fear — across nations, organizations, teams, and disciplines. Practice empathetic cross-cultural communication; counter ethnocentrism and projection.
- [Groupthink Mitigation (GTM)](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/groupthink-mitigation/) — Design facilitation so dissent is safe, alternatives are generated, and decisions are stress-tested before approval.
- [Self-Awareness](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/self-awareness/) — Know your own biases, temperament, and communication patterns before challenging others.
