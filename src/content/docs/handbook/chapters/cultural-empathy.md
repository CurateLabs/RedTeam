How culture shapes perception, cross-cultural communication, planning, and conflict — a core red teaming discipline, not an optional lens.

## Concepts & tools in this chapter

- [Ethnocentrism](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/ethnocentrism/) — Judging other cultures by the standards of one's own — the primary bias cultural empathy counteracts.
- [Operating Landscape Map](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/operating-landscape-map/) — Multi-domain map of the operating environment: power, security, economy, society, information, infrastructure, physical context, and time.
- [Kluckhohn's Six Dimensions](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/kluckhohns-six-dimensions/) — Age-old cultural variation axes for comparing cultural defaults (human nature, activity, time, relational, etc.).
- [Nisbett on Cognitive Differences](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/nisbett-on-cognitive-differences/) — How cognitive styles differ in attention, causality, and holism — affects planning and negotiation assumptions.
- [Hall on Communication Patterns](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/hall-on-communication-patterns/) — High-context vs. low-context communication; use of space and time — why the same words land differently.
- [Hofstede's Country Profiles](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/hofstedes-country-profiles/) — Power distance, uncertainty avoidance, individualism, masculinity/femininity, long-term orientation indices.
- [Five Operational Cultural Dimensions (historical)](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/five-operational-cultural-dimensions-historical/) — Source-handbook operationalization of cultural factors for planning in foreign environments.
- [Onion Model](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/onion-model/) — Culture manifests in layers: artifacts → espoused values → underlying assumptions.
- [Functional Systems Approach](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/functional-systems-approach/) — Analyze how cultural subsystems interact functionally rather than as isolated lists.
- [Cultural Perception Framework](/RedTeam/handbook/concepts/cultural-perception-framework/) — Structured method to surface how different actors perceive the same situation through their cultural lens.

## RedTeam note

Cultural empathy is a first-class principle. We extend it to organizational subcultures and stakeholder worlds without dropping the cross-cultural toolkit (Hall, Hofstede, Kluckhohn, perception frameworks). Operating Landscape Map is one tool among many.
