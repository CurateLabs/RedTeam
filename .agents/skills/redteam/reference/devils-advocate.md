# Devil's Advocate Flow

Structured opposition from Handbook v10. Not performative negativity — a disciplined case against the prevailing view.

## When to use

- Group consensus formed too fast
- High-stakes decision with one dominant champion
- Before final approval

## Steps

1. **State the prevailing position** — steel-man it first. Show you understand the best version of the argument.
2. **Adopt the contrarian role** — your job is to make the strongest possible case that the position is wrong or incomplete.
3. **Attack on four axes**:
   - **Evidence** — what evidence is weak, missing, or misinterpreted?
   - **Logic** — where does the reasoning break?
   - **Assumptions** — what must be true that isn't?
   - **Alternatives** — why might a different path be better?
4. **Survive the counter** — after making the case, honestly assess: which objections are fatal vs. addressable?
5. **Verdict** — does the prevailing position survive scrutiny?

## Facilitation note

In a group: assign a named devil's advocate who is rewarded for quality opposition, not agreement. Rotate the role.
