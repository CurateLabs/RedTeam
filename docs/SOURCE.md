# Source & lineage

RedTeam acknowledges its intellectual debt to the U.S. Army's applied critical thinking program while using **civilian language** in all product text, skills, and commands.

## Original source

| | |
|---|---|
| **Title** | *The Red Team Handbook*, Version 9.0 |
| **Publisher** | TRADOC G-2, University of Foreign Military and Cultural Studies (UFMCS) |
| **Program** | UFMCS taught structured red teaming to improve organizational decision-making |
| **Copyright** | U.S. Government work (17 U.S.C. § 105) |

We are **not** affiliated with the U.S. Army, TRADOC, or UFMCS. This project is an independent adaptation for civilian, product, and AI-assisted contexts.

## What we kept

- The idea of **loyal opposition** — challenge thinking to improve decisions, not to win arguments
- Structured techniques: premortems, competing hypotheses, assumption checks, frame audits, group facilitation patterns
- Emphasis on dissent, alternatives, and making implicit assumptions explicit
- **Cultural empathy** as a core discipline — cross-cultural communication, perception mapping, and empathetic inquiry

## Historical note (UFMCS)

The University of Foreign Military and Cultural Studies (UFMCS) was the U.S. Army program that developed and taught red teaming as **applied critical thinking** and **groupthink mitigation** — not as cybersecurity penetration testing. The handbook collected tools from decision science, facilitation practice, and cultural analysis into a single curriculum for planners and leaders.

RedTeam preserves that intent while removing dependency on military vocabulary in day-to-day use. When you see a technique in our catalog, it may trace to the handbook, to civilian decision science (Kahneman, Klein, Argyris, etc.), or to RedTeam-native extensions (AI-check, launch review, RFC review).
