# Chapter 1 — Foundational Concepts

Defines red teaming, the four core principles, and how techniques are organized in RedTeam.

## Concepts & tools in this chapter

- [Red Teaming](../concepts/red-teaming.md) — A structured cognitive approach that challenges plans and decisions to surface hidden risks, unseen options, and flawed assumptions before commitment.
- [UFMCS (historical)](../concepts/ufmcs-historical.md) — University of Foreign Military and Cultural Studies — the U.S. Army program that developed and taught the source handbook curriculum.
- [Four Core Principles](../concepts/four-core-principles.md) — ACT, cultural empathy, GTM, and self-awareness — the organizing frame for all commands.
- [Applied Critical Thinking (ACT)](../concepts/applied-critical-thinking.md) — Deliberately analyze how we perceive, interpret, and reason — make implicit assumptions explicit and test them.
- [Cultural Empathy](../concepts/cultural-empathy.md) — Understand how cultural frames shape what others see, value, and fear — across nations, organizations, teams, and disciplines. Practice empathetic cross-cultural communication; counter ethnocentrism and projection.
- [Groupthink Mitigation (GTM)](../concepts/groupthink-mitigation.md) — Design facilitation so dissent is safe, alternatives are generated, and decisions are stress-tested before approval.
- [Self-Awareness](../concepts/self-awareness.md) — Know your own biases, temperament, and communication patterns before challenging others.
