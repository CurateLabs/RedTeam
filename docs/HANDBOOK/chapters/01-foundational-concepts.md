# Chapter 1 — Foundational Concepts

Defines Red Teaming, UFMCS, and the four principles that organize every technique in the handbook and this project.

## Concepts & tools in this chapter

- [Red Teaming](../concepts/red-teaming.md) — A structured cognitive approach that challenges plans and decisions to surface hidden risks, unseen options, and flawed assumptions before commitment.
- [UFMCS](../concepts/ufmcs.md) — University of Foreign Military and Cultural Studies — the Army program that developed and taught Red Teaming methodology.
- [Four Principles of UFMCS](../concepts/four-principles-of-ufmcs.md) — The pillars every Red Team practice rests on: ACT, Cultural Empathy, GTM, and Self-Awareness.
- [Applied Critical Thinking (ACT)](../concepts/applied-critical-thinking.md) — Deliberately analyze how we perceive, interpret, and reason — make implicit assumptions explicit and test them.
- [Cultural Empathy](../concepts/cultural-empathy.md) — Understand how others' cultural frames shape what they see, value, and fear; avoid projecting your own frame.
- [Groupthink Mitigation (GTM)](../concepts/groupthink-mitigation.md) — Design group process so dissent is safe, alternatives are generated, and decisions are stress-tested before approval.
- [Self-Awareness & Reflection](../concepts/self-awareness-reflection.md) — Know your own biases, temperament, and communication patterns before trying to challenge others.
