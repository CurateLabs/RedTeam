# Chapter 2 — Self-Awareness & Reflection

How red teamers understand themselves — cognition, temperament, emotion, communication — before engaging others.

## Concepts & tools in this chapter

- [Self-Authorship](../concepts/self-authorship.md) — Developmental capacity to internally define beliefs, identity, and relationships rather than relying on external validation.
- [Temperament / Personality Dimensions](../concepts/temperament-personality-dimensions.md) — Jungian-based typology for understanding how people prefer to perceive and decide — useful for anticipating group friction.
- [Emotional Intelligence Framework](../concepts/emotional-intelligence-framework.md) — Self-competencies and social competencies that affect how we receive and deliver challenge.
- [Interpersonal Communication](../concepts/interpersonal-communication.md) — How messages are sent, received, and distorted — foundation for productive devil's advocacy without personal attack.
- [Interpersonal Conflict](../concepts/interpersonal-conflict.md) — Patterns of disagreement escalation; separate task conflict (healthy) from relationship conflict (destructive).
- [Introspection](../concepts/introspection.md) — Deliberate inward examination — journaling, reflection time, structured exercises.
- [Who Am I?](../concepts/who-am-i.md) — Introspective exercise: articulate identity, values, and biases before engaging others.
